# Listening and Active Communication

## Question 1
What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening?
- Avoid getting distracted by your own thoughts
- focusing on topic and speaker
- not interrupting pther person
- letting other person finish and then then responding 
- use of body language 
- taking notes if appropriate
---
## 2. According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening?
- mirroring body language
- Repeat back what you understood to confirm the message.
- Show empathy instead of disagreeing right away.
- Encourage them to share more by being supportive.
---
## 3. What are the obstacles in your listening process?
- Sometimes I am not interested in the topic.
- often i want to create a good impression so i start thinking about my reply while the other person is still talking.
---
## 4. What can you do to improve your listening?
- Show interest in the conversation.
- make eye contact.
- Ask questions whenever something's unclear.
- Practice listening more in daily life.
---
## 5. When do you switch to Passive communication style in your day to day life?
- When I’m not confident or don’t know enough about the thing that's being discussed
- When someone's angry and I just stay quiet.
- When I'm not confident about what's being discussed.
- When I think speaking up won’t change anything.
---
## 6. When do you switch into Aggressive communication styles in your day to day life?
- when i feel like i need to take a stand for myself.
- When I feel like other person is not even trying to understand my perspective.
- When I lose control of my emotions in heated situations.
---
## 7. When do you switch into Passive Aggressive (sarcasm/gossiping/taunts/silent treatment and others) communication styles in your day to day life?
- When I'm hurt but don't want to confront directly.
- When I feel resentful for always being passive and finally snap indirectly.
- When I feel like I can't speak openly about something.
---
## 8. How can you make your communication assertive?
- Keep steady eye contact and open posture.
- Speak at normal volume — no shouting, no whispering.
- Stay calm and confident while speaking.
- Ask directly for what I want instead of hinting.
- Listen fully without cutting them off.
